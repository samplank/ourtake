Community Success

Sliced is a news analysis platform where community members contribute and curate content together. The platform succeeds when we all buy into its core values of respect, inclusion, and truth.

We believe that access to top quality journalism is a central part of a healthy democracy. Because of this, all Sliced articles are always complety free to read. There is no paywall and we don't collect or sell user data.

We also are strongly committed to the idea that good journalism should be compensated. To ensure that community members receive payment when their contributions are insightful, the community financially supports good work.

How it Works

Sliced community members both read articles and create them together.

To dive into articles that other community members have collaborated on, stay on the Read tab.

When you want to contribute to article, sign in to the platform in the upper right on the page, then, click on the Contribute tab.

You will see the last three paragraphs of the article at the top. Below them, you will see contributions that are under consideration by the community. You can vote on these contributions. Any contribution that receives 10 upvotes within 3 hours of being proposed will be accepted into the article.

If you have something to add, write your own contribution, and let the community review it. To write your own contribution, you need to have cast at least 5 votes since the last time you wrote a contribution.

To review the accepted contributions, click on the Review tab.

There, you can see who wrote each contribution. You can also see how many upvotes and downvotes each contribution received.
